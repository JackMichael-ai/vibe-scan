ISO 10816-3 Velocity Thresholds — mm/s RMS

VibeScan reports in mm/s velocity so these values apply directly. The C++ engine integrates the FFT spectrum (divides each bin by 2πf × 1000) before computing RMS — not a raw acceleration reading.

Why the 0.4 Hz ghost is gone

The "ski-slope" leakage happens because the Hann window doesn't perfectly suppress a constant (gravity). Even after subtracting the per-window mean, the tapered centre samples carry a tiny residual. The fix: subtract the mean again after windowing. Then the peak detector ignores all bins below 2 Hz (the skip_bins threshold computed from actual sample rate). A stationary phone now correctly shows Peak Hz = 0.0.

Why 1024 samples not 512

Doubling the window from 512 to 1024 cuts frequency resolution from 0.39 Hz/bin to 0.195 Hz/bin. That means BPFO peaks are twice as sharp — a worn bearing at 89 Hz resolves into one clear spike instead of a blurry hill spanning 88–90 Hz. The 2 Hz skip zone now only discards 10 bins instead of 5, catching more real signal near 2 Hz.
